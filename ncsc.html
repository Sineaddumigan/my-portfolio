NCSC Website Redesign

As part of a talented team at Fluent interaction, I had the opportunity to contribute to the redesign of the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) website. This project aimed to modernize their online presence while ensuring accessibility, adaptability, and a cohesive visual identity. My primary role was focused on the visual design, with an emphasis on creating a system that would work seamlessly across a variety of scenarios and devices.

Visual Design and Adaptive Templates

Central to the redesign was developing a consistent and modern visual language. This involved crafting a cohesive color palette, selecting accessible typography, and creating a set of page layouts that adhered to a grid system for consistency and flexibility. The grid-based approach ensured that content was well-structured and adaptable across different screen sizes, from mobile to desktop.

We designed templates for key content types, including blogs, collections, guidance documents, case studies, events, galleries, and reports. Each template was built to handle varied content needs while maintaining a uniform visual identity. Collaboration with developers was critical during this phase to ensure the designs translated effectively into a responsive, functional website. We worked iteratively to refine the templates, addressing edge cases and ensuring every potential use case was accounted for.

Comprehensive Design System

To support the website's longevity and scalability, we developed a design system that encompassed:

  • Grid Systems: Defined layouts and spacing rules to maintain structure across all devices.
  • Color and Typography: Guidelines to ensure consistency and accessibility.
  • Modular Components: Reusable elements like buttons, forms, and icons to streamline development.

The design system not only provided clarity for developers but also ensured future updates could be implemented efficiently without compromising the visual identity.

Adaptive Design Across Devices

To meet the needs of modern users, we ensured the design was adaptive to four primary screen sizes: mobile, tablet, laptop, and desktop. Mockups for each size were created to test and optimize the user experience at every breakpoint. This approach guaranteed that the website was fully responsive and performed well regardless of the device.

Reflections on the Project

This project was an exciting challenge in visual design, requiring careful attention to detail and close collaboration across disciplines. It emphasized the importance of:

  • Designing for adaptability while maintaining visual cohesion.
  • Collaborating with developers to bridge the gap between design and implementation.
  • Ensuring that the design system and templates could handle all anticipated scenarios.
