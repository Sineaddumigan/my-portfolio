Working with the STA to create the first compulsory online digital assessment tool in the UK

The Multiplication tables check (MTC) is a compulsory online assessment that will be used to check primary 4 pupil’s ability to recall the times tables up to 12 x 12.

The idea was first put forward in a Conservative manifesto. The purpose is to improve the numeracy skills of pupils in the UK to aid their fluency in mathematics by helping teachers identify pupils who have not yet mastered this important mathematical skill so schools can give them additional support.

The Multiplication tables check works by displaying each question for an amount of time that allows for recall and not calculation.

What the team did

The team worked in Agile and engaged with the STA, test devleopers and schools across the UK to create a brand new service. There are 4 parts to the service:

  • Service manager to manage check windows, upload pupil data, edit time for questions in the check, manage settings for schools in different time zones
  • Test developers to upload and views forms, assign form to check windows and download pupil check data
  • Schools administration to register pupils, group pupils, attendance, access arrangements, mock area, generate passwords and PINs, restarts, Headteacher declaration form and see results
  • Pupils to take the ‘check’. Designing for a range of devices and a range of access arrangements.

Research was done to understand the different types of schools that exist across the UK, their digital capacities and any issues a digital assessment would impose on them. We then tested the prototype with the users and iterated on designs before building. We then tested the built service with users and continued to iterate designs when issues occured. We created a service that passed the GDS assessments and received 18/18 for the beta assessment.

Challenges

Understanding the technical landscape - Understanding the quality of connectivity that schools have and the situation of their ICT

Creating a service that adds as little burden as possible to schools - creating a service that is flexible enough to fit into schools’ busy schedules. Providing great UX so that teachers can complete tasks quickly with ease

Using the GOV.UK toolkit and adapting it for the use of children - designing using the GOV.UK toolkit and adapting them to create content and visuals that are appropriate for young children

Creating an assessment that is accessible for as many children as possible designing colour contrast, font configurations, in built screen readers etc so disabled pupils can independently take the check

An inclusive service

We tested the MTC with a range of user on a range of devices to ensure the service was as inclusive as possible. The devices we design for and tested with were mobile phones, tablets, latptops and desktops. We considered pupils with visual impairments, processing needs, physical disabilities, poor IT skills, pupils on the austistic spectrum, cognitive needs (e.g. dyslexia and dyscalculia) and English as an additional language. We designed the service to have the following access arrangements to include as many pupils as possible:

  • Colour and contrast
  • Font size
  • Questions reader
  • Audible time alert
  • Input assistant
  • Next question button (30 minute time limit)
  • Removal of onscreen number pad

Outcome

The project passed all the GDS assessments it has had so far, and will be rolled out to all schools in 2021 where schools can choose to administer the MTC to year 4 pupils before it becomes mandatory in the future.
