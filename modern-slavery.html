Designing a government-run service to increase transparency on organisations’ actions to elimitate modern slavery from their operations and supply chains

In 2015, it became a legal requirement for organisations with an annual turnover of £36 million or more to publish a modern slavery statement on their website. The purpose of the statements are to set out the risks of modern slavery and labour exploitation within an organisation’s operations and supply chains and the steps they are taking to prevent it.

In a consultation that launched soon after the legislation, repondents expressed the act would be more effective if the govenment launched a central repositry where all statements would be housed to further improve transparency and allow monitoring of compliance. In March 2020 we were contracted to deliver this service.

What we did

Worked in an Agile team using Scrum to deliver business value in the shortest time. This service was a close match to an existing service, Gender Pay Gap. The team reused parts of the codebase and redesigned other parts to allow a mature service to be delivered quickly. The project ran during the Pandemic and the team adapted to doing everything remotely. The research was done using Lookback, we used Mural to run workshops, Easy retro for team retros.

Asking about modern slavery risk

"Feels like it is a very thorough process. Adds an element of confidence that they are complying with legislation and that they are providing all the information that is required of them." Analyst

Challenges

Asking organisations about their modern slavery risks was one of the most complex and important areas of the service to design. The challenge was to ask questions that would allow organisations to express their modern slavery risks accurately and succinctly whilst gathering structured data to support analyst with their viewing, comparison and analysis. Identifying and sharing information on modern slavery risks increases transparency and understanding of where modern slavery is taking place, allowing investors, civil society and others to scrutinise the action taken and drive further change.

Outcome

The service is going to start off voluntary and is going to be rolled out the first week of March 2021 to 16,000 private organisations with a turnover of £36 million and over. We presented the service to key stakeholders e.g. Businesses Against Slavery and Independent Anti-Slavery Commisioner. So far it has been well received. The team and the Modern Slavery Unit feel positive about being a step forward in increasing transparency on modern slavery within organisation’s operations and supply chains and being an educational piece for businesses.
