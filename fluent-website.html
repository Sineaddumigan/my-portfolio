Creating the visual design for a digital agency's website

The companyâ€™s website had not been updated in over a decade, and as a digital agency, we recognized the importance of having a modern, representative online presence. Four of us took the initiative to redesign the site, starting with mid-fidelity mockups to test key user journeys and ensure usability. Once the flows were finalized, I took the lead on the visual design.

After conducting competitor reviews and holding workshops with our directors, I developed several homepage concepts. These were reviewed collaboratively with the team and directors to establish the overall design direction. To streamline the process, I created a comprehensive spreadsheet outlining all components and templates required for the site. I designed these in Sketch and exported them into Zeplin for seamless handoff to developers.

My role

I led the visual design efforts, focusing on the following areas:

  • UI Design and Art Direction: Established the visual identity and crafted a cohesive digital style guide.
  • UI Specifications: Defined precise design specifications for developers.
  • Accessibility Audit: Conducted an accessibility audit and collaborated with developers to ensure the site met accessibility standards.
  • User Flows: Created and refined key user journeys.
  • Icon Creation: Designed custom icons to complement the chosen design direction.
  • Adaptive Design: Designed for four screen widths, creating responsive layouts that adapted effectively across breakpoints.
  • Component Design: Built reusable components, documenting states, patterns, and behaviors to guide development.

Adaptive design

To ensure a seamless user experience across devices, I designed layouts for four screen widths, showcasing how the site would adapt at various breakpoints. These designs were created in Sketch and exported into Zeplin for developers to implement.

Icon creation

Custom icons were designed to align with the new visual direction, contributing to a modern and cohesive design system.

Components

Reusable components were meticulously designed, demonstrating various states and interactions. This approach standardized patterns and behaviors, making the development process more efficient.

Outcome

The redesigned website successfully went live, and the team unanimously felt that the new design better reflected the agency's values, capabilities, and identity. The project not only enhanced our online presence but also reinforced our commitment to delivering high-quality digital solutions.
